My Top 5 Favorite Ghanaian Meals

1. Jollof Rice

Main Ingredients:

  • Rice: Parboiled or Basmati Rice
    1. Stew Base: Fresh Tomatoes, Onions, Pepper (Scotch Bonnet or Kpakposhito)
    2. Flavour: Ginger, Garlic, Tomato Paste
    3. Liquid/Fat: Oil, Chicken or Beef Stock, Curry Powder, Thyme, Bay Leaves
  • Protein (Optional): Chicken, Beef, or Fish

2. Waakye (Rice and Beans)

Main Ingredients:

  • Grain: Rice
    1. Legume: Cowpeas or Black-eyed Beans
    2. Coloring: Dried Sorghum Leaves (Agbeli) or Baking Soda
  • Common Sides: Waakye Stew (Tomatoes/Onions), Shito (Black Pepper Sauce), Spaghetti, Boiled Egg, Fried Plantain (Kelewele)

3. Fufu and Light Soup (Nkakra)

Main Ingredients:

  1. Fufu: Cassava and Plantain (or Yam)
  2. Soup Base (Nkakra): Tomatoes, Onions, Pepper, Turkey Berries (Abeduru), Garden Eggs
  3. Protein: Goat Meat, Beef, Smoked Fish, or Snail
  4. Seasoning: Salt, Onion

4. Banku and Okro Stew/Soup

Main Ingredients:

  • Banku: Fermented Corn and Cassava Dough (Corn Flour, Cassava Dough, Water)
    1. Okro (Lady's Finger): Fresh Okro (sliced)
    2. Soup/Stew: Palm Oil, Onions, Tomatoes, Pepper, Fish (Tilapia, Mackerel) or Meat, Crab, Prawns
  • Seasoning: Salt, Smoked Fish Powder (Optional)

5. Kenkey and Fried Fish/Pepper

Main Ingredients:

  1. Kenkey: Fermented Corn Dough (White or Ga Kenkey)
    • Protein: Fried or Grilled Fish (e.g., Tilapia, Mackerel)
    • Sauce: Ground Pepper (Kpakposhito or Scotch Bonnet), Onions, Tomatoes, Ginger (Optional)
  2. Accompainment: Shito (Black Pepper Sauce), Sliced Onions, Fried Egg (Optional)

Detailed Preparation for Two Meals

Preparation of Jollof Rice

The preparation of Jollof Rice starts with the Stew Base. Onions are saut√©ed, and a blend of pureed **Tomatoes, Pepper, Ginger, and Garlic** is added along with **Tomato Paste**. This mixture is fried down until the oil floats on top (a crucial step to remove the sourness of the tomatoes). Next, Stock (chicken or beef) and seasonings (Thyme, Curry Powder, Bay Leaves) are added to the stew and brought to a boil. The Rice is then thoroughly washed to remove excess starch, added to the boiling stew, and stirred well to ensure every grain is coated. The pot is covered tightly (often with foil and a lid) and allowed to steam on low heat until the rice is tender and the liquid is absorbed. This steaming process infuses the smoky, rich stew flavor into the rice, making it a distinct "Ghana Jollof."

Preparation of Fufu and Light Soup (Nkakra)

The preparation involves two concurrent tasks: making the fufu and preparing the soup.

Part 1: Preparing the Fufu

Cassava and Plantain (or sometimes Yam) are peeled, boiled until tender, and drained. Traditionally, the boiled pieces are transferred into a large wooden mortar and pounded vigorously with a pestle until a smooth, elastic, and homogenous dough (**Fufu**) is formed. Water is occasionally added to maintain the desired consistency. The finished dough is then molded into smooth, rounded balls.

Part 2: Preparing the Light Soup (Nkakra)

A smooth, watery stew is created. Onions, Tomatoes, and Pepper** are boiled with the Meat/Protein (e.g., goat meat). Once the meat is tender, the vegetables are removed, blended, and returned to the pot to thicken the broth. The soup is seasoned with just Salt (sometimes onion) and allowed to simmer until the flavors deepen. Turkey Berries** and **Garden Eggs** may be added whole for subtle flavor. The soup is served hot, and the Fufu is eaten by tearing off small lumps with the fingers and dipping them into the soup.
